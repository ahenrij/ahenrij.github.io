Hi! I'm Henri Aïdasso, born and raised in Benin, a humble place that taught me the value of adaptability, efficiency, and hard work. Over the past decade, I've studied across three continents and worked across a dozen organizations of all sizes: startups, government institutions, research centers, and global corporations. I recently completed a PhD in Computer Engineering, with grade Excellent, at École de technologie supérieure (ÉTS), University of Quebec. During my PhD, I was also a Mitacs Accelerate Fellow at TELUS. My research focuses on the reliability of multi-component automated systems, specifically anomaly detection and diagnosis in software and AI systems using efficient machine learning methods. My work bridges fundamental AI research with real-world impact.
